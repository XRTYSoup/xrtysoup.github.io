What is Aquaponics?

Aquaponics is known to be a system of growing water-based animals and fishes including land plants in such a controlled environment. The purpose of this is to maximize the nutrients and energy used in the entire system and to harvest a lot of fish protein and vegetables.

Actually, aquaponics comes from the words aquaculture and hydroponics wherein the former is about the cultivation of water-based animals and fish while the latter is where plats are cultivated in water or in a sterile medium.

Actually, it was in China that Aquaponics first became known while other aquaponics system parts were developed in other countries. There were people who became interested in observing the relationships which have existed in the environment.

Aquaponics has also been explored in the past few years as the best solution to food shortage, energy and environmental issues. This is because this best combines the method of growing fish or aquatic-based animals (aquaculture) and hydroponics as the modern technology growing plants without soil.

In aquaponics, it actually follows the method of growing plants and fishes in such a closed loop system with water loss or low rate of water because of evaporation. As per the effluent or fish waste, it is to be delivered right from the tank to another settling tank. This way, the heavy waste will be removed and will be sent to the trays as a source of food for all those growing plants. The plants will now provide a filter in order that the water will keep the fish healthy.

In this regard, a symbiotic relationship will be established between the growing plants and the fish which served as an ultimate goal of aquaponics. A more sustainable ecosystem will be created wherein plants and fish can live and can thrive. This will now result to a more productive result such as healthy vegetables and fresh protein.

However, it must not also be forgotten that nitrifying bacteria or microbes are another third participant to consider. They convert ammonia that comes from the fish waster into nitrites while the solids into vermicompost. The latter is the perfect foods for those plants.

If you will compare aquaponics with the conventional soil-based gardening, the latter has a lot of problems faces such as amount of water needed, weeds, soil-borne insects, raccoons, four-legged pests and bunnies. Apart from it, there is a need to know the right time to water and the ways to fertilize. Even the soil composition must also be clearly known from the very start.

With hydroponics, these problems or issues will be resolved since this does not rely much on the application of man-made and expensive nutrients. There is no need to mix concoction of trace elements, salts and chemicals. There is just however, a need to feed the fish with inexpensive food scraps, fish feeds and grown foods.

Even the strength of the mixture no longer needs careful and consistent monitoring. The aquaponics will only need to be monitored during the 1st month. After it has been established clearly, there is a need to check ammonia and pH levels on a weekly basis. This is also in case that you have found your fish or plants stressed.

As compared to hydroponic systems, the water in aquaponics does not need to be replaced. There is only a need to top it off when it evaporates. In terms of diseases, aquaponics is not virtually exposed to diseases such as root rot or “Pythium”.

Types of Aquaponics Systems

Rather than utilizing toxic chemical solutions or dirt to grow plants, this system utilizes a nutritious fish with all the necessary nutrients needed for t plant development. Rather than discharging water, this system utilizes the plants as well as the media in which they cultivate to purify and clean the water.

Afterwards, it is now brought back to the tank. The water could still be re-used for an indefinite period of time. It will just need to be substituted after it has been lost in evaporation and transpiration. Two main techniques of growing Aquaponics are popularly utilized today.

Raft or Deep Flow

This type of Aquaponics is popularly utilized for commercial purposes. Raft or deep flow system utilized a floating raft that is made of a floating board or Styrofoam with holes for the pots in order that the plants will sit properly.

In a raft system, raft sits in water that is alienated from the fish tank. The raft serves like a bio-filter of the water ravages from the tank. Through this kind of system, you will realize that when you feed your fish on it, the more plants will grow. Now, if you have enough room for planting, ensure to have an individual bio-filter in holding the waste from the tank.

Media based Aquaponics

It is popularly created for the yard Aquaponics system wherein one can see it even if he or she is far away. It utilized clay or pebbles for drainage and for cleaning purposes. The pebbles within the plant beds force in the directive of water flooding in the drainage. This is also to ensure that it flows constantly. This is also kept away from any obstruction that only interrupts the work of the system. But, the clay in the plant beds absorbs the water to keep the roots of the plants. This helps maintain its dampness without getting soaked up.

Nutrient Film Technique or NFT

Another kind of Aquaponics system to use on either commercial or backyard system is the Nutrient Film Technique or NFT for short. On the other hand, it is seldom used. In this method, plants are placed in the pots. Only a little amount of water seeps from the bottom of the pots. This is also where the roots could access a small amount of water from it.

Indoor Aquaponics system

This system can help grow vegetables and plants while it cleans the water in the fish tank. It is considered as one of the most renowned Aquaponics system. People could make use of the typical aquarium.

However, just like other kinds of systems, you must be careful in dealing with plants and fishes. There must be a constant checking, a right filtration and a right water circulation. It utilized a five centimeter layer of river gravel beds technique for filtration. This helps filter the waste of the fish and let the good bacteria grow. You can utilize plants that no longer need direct sunlight. Thus, the fish could also benefit more from it as well.

Hybrid Aquaponics System

This is a combination of media and raft system. Media beds serve as the filter of the waste prior to the water that comes in the raft system. This style is the center of “The Aquaponics Source’s AquaBundance” systems as it offers its planting flexibility, low maintenance and high productivity.

All kinds of Aquaponics systems are ideal to each kind of home. But, it does not matter if you have a big or small space in your home. This is for as long as that it fits your purpose of setting it up- commercial or home purposes.

If you like to use it commercially, then you must have a huge place to install your system. But all in all, it is the cheapest way of growing fish and plants. You will also be promised of huge harvests in time.

By using these interdependent subsystems, the Aquaponics technique facilitates the farming of plants and aquatic life forms in a limited space utilizing purely organic techniques.
